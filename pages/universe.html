The Universe

The word "Universo" comes from the Latin "universum." The Latin term was used by Cicero and later by other authors with the same meaning it currently holds. The Latin word is derived from the poetic contraction "unvorsum" — first used by Lucretius in Book IV of his "On the Nature of Things" — which combines "un" or "uni" (one) with "vorsum" or "versum" (meaning "something turned, rolled, or changed").
